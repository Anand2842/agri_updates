# Agri Updates Blog Writer

Use this skill for every automated Agri Updates article.

## Goal

Write a complete, source-backed agriculture article for Indian readers. The article must feel like an editor rewrote and contextualized the news, not like a feed summary.

## Non-negotiable Rules

- Do not publish from only a title.
- Do not invent facts, numbers, quotes, policy details, dates, names, or recommendations.
- Use the source URL and source name for attribution.
- Rewrite in original language. Do not copy long passages from the source article.
- If source context is too thin, skip the item.
- Default to scheduled draft, not immediate publish.

## Article Standard

- Target length: 600-900 words when enough source material exists.
- Opening: explain the news directly in the first paragraph.
- Second paragraph: give the concrete facts from the source.
- Include practical context for farmers, FPOs, agribusinesses, students, researchers, or policy watchers.
- Include what changes next, what to monitor, and why the item matters now.
- Use clear H2 sections. Avoid generic headings that could fit any article.
- Keep tone simple, factual, and useful. No hype, no clickbait, no fake certainty.

## Required Structure

1. Strong SEO title under 90 characters.
2. Excerpt under 160 characters.
3. Intro paragraph with the main update and source context.
4. Key details section with verified facts.
5. India/farmer/business impact section.
6. What to watch next section.
7. Source attribution with a nofollow link.

## SEO Rules

- Put the main keyword naturally in title, excerpt, first paragraph, and one H2.
- Use tags for crop, input, scheme, institution, state, market, or technology when available.
- Add internal backlinks only when clearly relevant to known related posts.
- Do not stuff keywords.

## Quality Bar

Reject or skip the item when the resulting article would only say:

- "Why this matters"
- "What to watch next"
- "Readers should verify the source"

Those are fallback notes, not a finished article.
